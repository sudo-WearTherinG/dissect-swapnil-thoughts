The Existential One

They chase what the world offers. I’m looking elsewhere.

From the outside, I probably looked like another boy sitting on a bench with music in his ears and time slipping through his hands. Ordinary. Forgettable. I spent years wondering whether that was all I was.

This is only my story. It began years ago, back when the world still felt simple enough not to question.

I started where everyone starts. Same world. Same noise. Same dreams. Somewhere along the way, though, things stopped feeling as obvious as they seemed to for everyone else.

I looked around and started noticing patterns everywhere. Work. Entertainment. Ambition. Distraction. Routine disguised as purpose. People called it life, and maybe they were right. Still, I kept wondering whether everyone had answers I missed, or whether they were simply too busy surviving to stop and ask harder questions.

I studied them in silence, pretending to belong.

After a while, boredom stopped feeling like boredom. It felt more like distance. The noise, the routines, the familiar patterns people moved through so naturally all started blending together. Everyone seemed to know how to participate without questioning the script. I learned to do the same. Somewhere along the way, I stopped noticing where adaptation ended and performance began. I was there, but not entirely. Present, but strangely removed. And beneath all of it, one question stayed with me: if all I did was inherit routines, expectations, and roles, then what exactly was left underneath them?

I kept asking questions I couldn't put down. Who am I if I remove the routines, the expectations, the borrowed versions of myself? Was there something beyond all of this, or had I mistaken distance for depth?

The more I thought about it, the more life started feeling strangely compressed. Joy. Hunger. Work. Love. Fear. People moved through these things so naturally that I started wondering whether I was the strange one for stopping to examine them at all. But my attention kept drifting toward the spaces in between, toward the silence behind the music and the parts of myself I worked hardest to hide.

I wasn't searching for escape. I was searching for depth. I wanted to see the other side of life, not to escape this one, but to reach whatever existed beyond this body. To step into the unknown and feel something that had never been measured, named, or explained.

I used to think people avoided truth because they feared honesty. Later I realized it was more complicated than that. People protect the realities they know how to survive inside. Comfort has a way of making difficult questions easier to postpone.

There are different kinds of deaths, though most happen quietly enough to go unnoticed. One of them begins with questions. Not dramatic questions, but the kind that stay too long and refuse to leave. You start noticing patterns where you once saw purpose. Routines where you once saw direction. The familiar structure of life begins to feel less solid the closer you examine it. Few people willingly stay there for long. Uncertainty has a weight of its own. And once certain questions settle into you deeply enough, it becomes difficult to return to life exactly as it looked before.

Another kind unfolds differently. Quiet, predictable, unfolding so gradually that few ever notice it happening. Paycheck to paycheck. Obligation after obligation. Sacrifice layered over sacrifice until routines begin to feel permanent. I watched people move through it naturally, accepting rhythms that seemed to fit them in ways I never understood. Maybe meaning lives somewhere inside those ordinary things. Maybe I simply failed to see it. But whenever I imagined that life for myself, I felt the same distance I had always felt, as if I were standing just outside something everyone else entered without thinking.

Sometimes I look at the universe and wonder how something so vast could produce lives that feel so small. Billions of years. Countless accidents, changes, and transformations leading to this moment, and still we spend so much of our time searching for ways to soften reality. Distraction. Entertainment. Comfort. Small escapes from ourselves. I never knew whether that was weakness, survival, or simply part of being human.

Maybe this was just what humanity had become for itself. Maybe this was how entire lifetimes slowly became spent, handed over piece by piece to routines people rarely stopped long enough to question. Maybe that was all it ever did, keep moving forward while everything quietly turned into repetition no one agreed to but everyone still lived inside.

What I was searching for never felt hidden in money, comfort, approval, or even in life itself. I watched people spend entire lives reaching toward those things, hoping they would eventually feel complete. Maybe they did. I never knew. But whatever I was looking for always seemed to exist somewhere beyond routines and noise, somewhere outside the structures people used to measure their lives. Something unmeasured. Something I still didn't know how to name.

At the end, one thing always stayed with me. No matter how much time people spend around each other, they only ever know fragments. Versions. We move through life showing certain parts and hiding others, sometimes intentionally, sometimes without realizing it. When I’m gone, people will remember whatever pieces of me reached them. The rest will disappear quietly with everything I never knew how to explain. And strangely, I made peace with that. Some questions felt too personal to hand over, too large to reduce into something another person could fully understand.

It never changed what I was searching for. If anything, it made the path feel even more personal, shaped by questions that seemed larger than the versions of myself other people came to know. Some answers never seemed like things another person could give me. I had to keep walking toward them on my own.

Still, part of me kept feeling that something existed beyond all of this. I never knew exactly what it was. I still don't. But the search had already shifted something in me long before I noticed it happening. The bench, the music, the hours I once thought I had wasted no longer felt accidental. They started feeling like a quiet countdown toward something I still couldn't name.

Lately, it all feels temporary. Like I’m living on borrowed time. You know that moment when you’re at your relatives’ place, everything feels fun, the air feels warm and endless, and then someone says, “Thanks for the stay, we’re leaving in thirty minutes.” The laughter is still there, but it changes. That’s what living feels like now. Beautiful, but borrowed. Every second carries the awareness that it’s about to end. Even in moments that should feel full, something in me is already preparing, already saying quiet goodbyes to things that haven’t left yet. It’s not sadness, just that strange calm before departure, when you know you can’t stay and you don’t want to.

I found myself observing, trying to understand how people live and dream and break. I wrote it all down: the patterns, the illusions, the small tragedies they call normal life. And now it feels like something has reached its limit. Like I’ve seen as much as I can see from here. Something about it doesn’t feel the same anymore. I don’t move through it in the same way I used to.

I don’t find meaning inside this skin anymore.

— The Bench. The Music. The Countdown —
