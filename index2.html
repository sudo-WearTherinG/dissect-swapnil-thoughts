A Monologue

Just a Father and His Child Bonding

Ever thought about your dad as a child? Yes, he was a child too. Ever wondered why he goes through so much to provide for your demands? And he completes them without question: not as a responsibility, but love.

You give that big wide smile when you get what you asked for, that sparkle in the eyes: and as boy, I'll be honest, we're suckers for it. Maybe he remembers being a kid too, asking his own father for small things, and now he smiles to himself when he sees you do the same.

When he gets his anger out on you, remember it's not you but something happened at work place or maybe in train or maybe it was a small build-up of stress, somewhere. Just cut him some slack and don't talk back on him, he'll realize it when he cools down.

He might not say “I love you” every day, but he shows it every time he quietly fixes a problem before you even know it existed. And all he ever asks for in return is that smile. That one smile that makes it all worth it.


You Were Never Meant to Be Rich

You spent 30, maybe 40 years of your life in that company, day in and day out, gave them your youth, your energy, your health: Then why aren’t you rich? Why don’t you have the luxury you once dreamed of: the nicer car, the bigger house, the freedom? the vacation home, the designer clothes? Shouldn’t loyalty pay off? Shouldn’t sacrifice come back around?

But no, the system was never designed to make you rich. It was designed to use you like a cow: quiet, consistent, replaceable. The milk is your effort, your time, your sweat: and to them, that milk is profit. They gave you just enough to keep you working, fed you promises to keep you hopeful, and expected you to keep producing.

They never planned to let you live well: they planned to use you well. Promotions? Just things to dangle in front of you to keep you going. Benefits? Just enough to stop you from getting tired or quitting. And when you can’t work anymore, they don’t thank you. They don’t honour you. They give you a small pension, maybe a handshake, and forget about you the next day.

Because to them, you weren’t a person. You were an asset. A number. A cow that gave milk: and when the milk ran out, so did your worth.


Paying Taxes, Getting Nothing in Return

Let’s talk about public services. The idea is simple: you pay taxes, and in return, the government is supposed to provide you with services that improve your life: better healthcare, quality education, a reliable transportation system, and proper infrastructure. But look around. How many times have you waited in long queues at government hospitals only to be told there are no beds or no medicine? How many schools are overcrowded, underfunded, with teachers who can barely get by themselves? Roads and public transport barely hold together, crumbling under the weight of neglect, while you continue paying taxes that supposedly fund these services.

You walk into a government hospital with a sick family member, and you’re told there are no beds. Medicines? Out of stock. Cleanliness? Non-existent. You wait for hours just to be seen. In schools, you’re promised education, but the classrooms are overcrowded, the teachers are underpaid and demotivated, and the facilities are falling apart. Your children are told to dream big while sitting on broken benches with outdated books.

You drive on pothole-filled roads that damage your vehicle, waste your time, and risk your life. You try taking a bus or train, and it’s either late, overcrowded, or falling apart. In an emergency, the system expects you to adjust, not the other way around.

You pay for clean water, yet buy water cans because the taps at home deliver poison. You pay for electricity and still face blackouts. You pay for safety but feel unsafe walking home at night. You pay tax after tax: from income, GST, tolls, cess, and fuel: and still have to arrange everything on your own. Private hospitals, private schools, private security: because the public version has collapsed. The most basic utilities: water, electricity, sanitation, waste disposal: are either missing or broken. These are the things your taxes are supposed to cover, yet you end up paying twice: once to the government, and again to private providers.

You don’t see the returns on what you contribute. You don’t get the luxury of comfort or the peace of mind that your needs are taken care of. Instead, you’re stuck in a system where your hard-earned money is diverted to things that don’t reach you. The politicians, the corporations, and those at the top continue to live their lives in comfort, while the system is only there to serve those already in power, not the people who built this country with their own hands. They take from you, and they give back little or nothing in return.

You're Like A Fucking Rabbit Who Breeds For Them

You’ve spent your life working, paying taxes, doing your part, while the government takes what it can. But what does it give back? Nothing. It’s like paying alimony to a divorced wife who doesn’t even care about you anymore. You give and give, but nothing comes back: just empty promises and empty hands. You’re stuck in a cycle, supporting a system that doesn’t give a damn about you. It drains you, then leaves you to struggle on your own. And what do you do? You get married, have kids, and teach them the same broken system, passing on the same false hopes, while they too become pawns in this never-ending game. They’ll keep the cycle alive, just like you did.

They want you quiet. Exhausted. Constantly busy. Confused. So you never ask the hard questions. So you don’t see what’s being taken from you. So you just keep giving: until there’s nothing left.

Am I The Only One Who’s Awake?

Sometimes I fucking wonder if I’m the last real human left. Everyone else? NPCs with dead eyes and fake smiles. Programmed to wake up, work, eat, scroll, sleep: repeat. No questions, no rage, no soul. It’s like walking through a graveyard of warm bodies. How the fuck are they not losing their minds? How do they not see they’re slaves in a cage dressed up like freedom? Either they’re fucking blind… or they love the damn leash.
