A Monologue

Maybe The Answers I Seek Are Not Here.

What “Success” Means Here vs There? They say you made it if you have the car, the title, the lifestyle. But that’s only here. That’s success in a world built on illusions. What about there, in the after-life, if it’s even true? What if success for the soul isn’t about what you gain, but about what you no longer need? What if it’s about walking away lighter, emptier, freer? If you still need to hold on to something, if you’re still bound, maybe you’re coming back here again, until you learn to let go.

What if You’re Close to the End? Sometimes I wonder... maybe the reason I’m even thinking like this, questioning all of it, is because I’m close. Not close like dying, but close to understanding. Close to cracking the code. What if the moment you start asking “why am I here?” that’s when you’re finally ready to leave?

I know, it might sound like a fairytale. Something that doesn’t quite fit with the reality we know. But there’s this feeling I can’t shake. A quiet truth, almost hidden, that makes me think maybe it's not all just random. Maybe the real question isn’t about what you’ve done in this life, but about what you’re ready to let go of. And if you think this is just a delusion of mine, then explain this: why do we keep gods in our homes? Why pray, offer, and hold onto something if none of it matters?
