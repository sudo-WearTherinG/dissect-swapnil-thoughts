Lessons We Refuse to Learn

I'm Chasing Meaning, Not Money.

Why do humans chase money? Well, to get a better lifestyle, right? But I think differently. I often ask myself: Why do we exist in the first place? And what happens after death? No one has any idea. I once asked my sister, and she said, “You’re either burned to dust, vaporized, or become worm food.” I said, “Yes, that’s true for the body. But after that... what happens?”

We know death is unavoidable. You take nothing with you, and all you leave behind are the memories and the things you’ve gained. From here, we have two paths. One is the view my sister holds: that after death, there’s nothing. The other is the path Shree Krishna talks about: that life is a cycle, and death isn’t the end, but a transition to something beyond.

I’m agnostic. I don’t believe in your Gods, not the ones humans created. People go there to beg, to ask for favors, or when they’re in financial trouble. It’s always the same: always asking for something, always expecting something in return. No one really goes to them for their presence; it’s always to make a complaint, to ask for help, or to demand some sort of favor. It’s like a transactional relationship; one-sided and selfish.

I never asked them for anything, because I don’t believe in them that way. I don’t even pray to them. For me, it comes from within myself. I don’t need to fold my hands or chant words. I speak to them with my heart, my eyes, and my inner voice. That’s my way.

Lord Krishna talked about life. That this human experience is temporary, an illusion, something to see through. He spoke about the soul, about how this body isn’t you; it’s just something you’re wearing for now. What matters is what’s behind it. And to find that, you have to look beyond your wants, your image, your attachments. Because the truth isn’t in what you gain, but in what you let go of.

Your soul keeps changing bodies. That’s what they say. But I always wondered: why? And I think I’ve found the answer. It’s simple, really. In a sense, both the body and the soul are cursed. The body suffers through the hardships of life, and the soul suffers by being forced to move from one body to another. Not because it wants to, but because it hasn’t learned how to break free or let it go...

So this makes me wonder: why does this keep happening to us? If the soul doesn’t want to return, then why must it? Maybe it’s not punishment, but something unfinished. Something we keep missing. Like a loop we can’t escape because we haven’t understood the lesson. Not just about right and wrong, but about letting go. Of anger, of pride, of wanting. Maybe that’s the only way out.

Maybe it’s not the answers we find, but the questions we learn to live with.
