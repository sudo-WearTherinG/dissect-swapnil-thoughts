Born. Used. Discarded.

Look around. What do you see?

A body. Worn down over time, quietly and without ceremony. Muscles that tire. A mind that gets used. The system doesn't announce what it takes. It just keeps running, and you keep showing up, and somewhere in the middle of that arrangement something gets spent that nobody formally agreed to give.

The signs everywhere say the same thing. Dream big. Nobody specifies what gets spent in the process. Attention. Time. The quieter parts of yourself that go first without announcement. Somewhere along the way validation becomes the point. Likes, follows, small confirmations that you exist and matter. People trade truth for something that feels close enough to it. Comfort. The edge stays numb because numb is easier to maintain.

The noise doesn't stop. It just changes shape. Conversations, feeds, opinions, all of it moving fast enough that nobody has to sit with silence too long. Silence is where the uncomfortable questions live. So people fill it. With sound, with scrolling, with each other. Love works the same way sometimes. Two people filling something in each other. It holds for a while. Then the effort shows. Then the loop continues.

The job doesn't need to break you. It just needs to keep you busy enough that breaking feels like a personal failure rather than a design feature. You come back tomorrow because stopping costs more than continuing. That's the arrangement. It doesn't require your enthusiasm. Just your presence.

The body adapts to almost anything. Alarm clocks. Commutes. Stress. Artificial light. Sitting for hours. Standing for hours. It learns the rhythm demanded of it. That's the impressive part. The frightening part is how easily adaptation gets mistaken for health. Just because something survives doesn't mean it was meant to live that way.

You’re born. You’re taught how to behave.

You serve the machine. You die.

Roll credits.

Something in how it runs doesn't match something in how I'm built. I can see the machinery underneath it. I can't stop seeing it. I see the cogs turning, the lies feeding the hunger, the slow decay disguised as living.

The strange thing is how obedient the body remains. It keeps breathing. Keeps healing. Keeps carrying you through routines you never questioned and destinations you never chose.

I don’t belong here. Not really. Not fully.

Because this doesn't feel like living. It feels like motion. The same faces moving through the same hours, holding things that don't quite fill the space they're meant to fill. I watch it and can't find the part of myself that's supposed to join in.

Everyone seems to need something to hold onto. Dreams. Love. Titles. Anything that keeps the motion feeling like direction. I watch how tightly people grip these things. Not with judgment. Just with a quiet kind of recognition that I don't feel the same pull.

I looked for meaning and found machinery.
