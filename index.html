PROLOGUE

I came here to study. To understand. To look beneath the surface.

What I found was not what I expected.

This world, this so-called civilization, is a patchwork of broken promises and quiet desperation.
A system built on habits, rituals, and illusions.

Progress? Maybe.
But progress towards what?

They sell comfort, success, meaning, but all I see are shadows playing on walls.
People chasing ghosts in crowded rooms.

I’m an outsider looking in.
A student of a society obsessed with degrees, salaries, and status, none of which I ever cared for.

No fanfare. No grand revelations.
Just quiet observations and a sinking feeling.

This is what the world offers.
Nothing more. Nothing less.

And to the ones still clinging-
To the 9-to-5 grind parent,
chasing promotions like gods,
hoping purpose waits at the end of the week.
To the believers of systems,
rules, ranks, rituals-
who mistake obedience for progress.
To those who keep going
not because they want to,
but because they forgot how to stop.

*I’m not judging.*
*I’m just watching.*
*And wondering if they see it too.*

Humans mimic concern.
But the data shows detachment.
Patterns matter more than people.

I don’t feel like I belong here.
But I’ve learned to watch from the edges — just waiting to fall.
