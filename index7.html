Incapable of Loving Or Loved

Ghost And Mess Can't Be Together. Period.

I didn’t just end up like this.

My parents fought all the time. Like it was normal. Like they liked it. Well, mostly dad. I was just stuck in the middle, the thing they used to hurt each other.

Love, to me, never looked like hugs or kind words. It looked like silence, guilt trips, fake smiles at dinner. That’s what I grew up thinking love was.

So yeah, maybe that’s why I overthink everything now. Why I break down every word like it’s a trap. Because it used to be.

Still is, sometimes.

Love? I loved once. When the feeling hit before the meaning did. Maybe I didn’t even fully get what love meant then, just a kid caught in a feeling bigger than me. Since then? Nothing. No repeats. No reruns. Not because I’m numb. Because I know better. I don’t want skin. I want soul connection. Most people are hollow in all the wrong places.

If a girl ever said “I love you, Swapnil,” I wouldn’t know what to do with it. Not because I’d think she was lying. Because some part of me would immediately start searching for the catch. Looking for the hidden condition. The thing that disappears later. Maybe that says more about me than it does about her.

Going into a relationship is like giving away parts of yourself. When it breaks, you lose yourself. You can’t pick those pieces back up or glue them back together either. And before you know it, you become like them. Doing the same to someone else. Breaking them like you were broken. Me? I still have all my parts.

Maybe growing up changed how I looked at things too. Because it's true. They made motherhood a product. Fatherhood a payment plan. Childhood a countdown. And love? Love’s just an insurance policy against loneliness now.

Maybe I started seeing love differently after a while. Sometimes I wonder how much of it is connection and how much of it is simply two lonely people trying not to carry life alone. People searching for someone to make life feel lighter, quieter, easier to survive. Maybe they're the same thing. Maybe they aren't. I honestly don't know. I just know love never looked simple from where I was standing. I never really learned how to give pieces of myself away the way other people seem to. I kept my pieces. I’m still 97% me. And no, I’m still not talking about the missing 3%.

It’s simple.

I didn’t grow up around love. I grew up around fights. Guilt. Silence. I learned early: love can be loud, sharp, and dangerous. That’s why I hold back. Why I stay distant. Why I keep all my pieces. Because once, I was just a kid in the crossfire.

I tell myself it isn’t loneliness if I chose it. Maybe that's true. Maybe that's just the version of the story that's easier to live with.

And now?

Now I don’t call it trauma. I just call it... remembering.

Maybe this is what happens after enough years pass. You stop calling it damage. You stop calling it trauma. Eventually it just starts feeling like personality.

Back then, it would’ve felt like vanishing. Now, it just feels like fading.

I can’t do relationships. Trusting someone enough to break me? That’s a game trauma taught me to quit.

Because I don’t think I miss people the way others do. I miss the version of them that only existed with me. The conversations. The moments. The small pieces that disappeared when they did. When it ends, I don’t really move forward. I leave parts of myself behind. I stay in those flames longer than I should.

They got curious. Then they got bored. Me? I stayed. Still holding the pieces they never knew they left behind.

I’m just a ghost running on coffee, long since stopped trying to be part of the living.

I try to be kind to everyone so no one sees that I’m not actually here.

And yeah, I still smile. Sometimes.

I stay behind and build things people will probably never understand. Maybe that's what I do with the pieces people leave behind. I turn them into something. Pages. Thoughts. Fragments. Proof that something happened here.
