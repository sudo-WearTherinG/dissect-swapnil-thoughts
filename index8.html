Detached, Disconnected

I Thought It Was Fun, Then I Asked Why.

There’s a strange kind of distance I live in. Not physical. Emotional. Mental. Maybe even spiritual. Like I was dropped into this world not to belong, but to quietly study it. I don’t hate it. I’m not angry. I just see it differently. And the more I observe, the clearer it gets. Not everyone is built for the same kind of joy.

Sometimes I sit quietly and watch people scream on roller coasters. They laugh, shout, film it like it’s the highlight of their lives. They wait in line, pay money, call it “fun.” But I don’t see fun. I see controlled fear, followed by a burst of chemicals in the brain. Dopamine. Adrenaline. A spike. A release. That’s all it is. A biological high. It passes, and they go home saying it was the best part of their day.

We chase these highs through lights, noise, movement, crowds. Again and again. But beneath it, it’s always the same reward system. Different triggers. Same loop. One person finds it on a ride. Another in a festival crowd. Another in shopping. I find it in silence. In music. In being alone. And no, that doesn’t make me sad or broken. It just means I see the mechanism behind the experience.

I’ve never really been able to join the rush. Even when I’m physically present, my mind stands at a distance. While the moment is happening, I’m already questioning it. What made this fun? Why do we crave it? Why do we build entire weekends around chasing temporary spikes of joy? Is it joy at all, or just the absence of boredom?

That’s where the disconnect begins. Not from hate or bitterness. Just a different wiring. I’ve realized I’m not built like most. I don’t crave the same things. I don’t react the same way. And it’s hard to explain without sounding arrogant, but I’ll say it this way.

I’m like caviar. You don’t know what it tastes like. Not because you're lesser, but because you've never needed to. Not everyone understands it, because not everyone is meant to. It’s salty. Quietly bitter. An acquired taste. Some get it. Some never will.

And that’s fine. Because I’m not here to be understood. I’m not here to chase what others chase. I’ve tried that. I’ve smiled in crowded rooms, said the right words, played the role. But while everyone else was celebrating, I was already stepping back. Noticing how rehearsed it all felt.

People say I overthink. That I should just let go. But I don’t think I’m overthinking. I think I’m simply not distracted. Most people are too caught up in the moment to see the pattern. I can’t not see it. My wiring is different. And once you see behind the curtain, it becomes impossible to unsee.

It’s not that I don’t feel. It’s that I feel differently. What excites others doesn’t move me. What gives me peace often goes unnoticed. Maybe that makes me invisible in rooms filled with noise. Or maybe I just prefer the silence over applause.

So I watch. I observe. Not with judgment, but with clarity. While most are chasing the next spark, I’m standing still. Watching the system they don’t even realize they’re part of.

I’m not detached because I’m cold. I’m detached because I’ve seen the script.

I don’t live life. I simulate it.

Some drift a little farther from the surface, not out of choice but instinct. They sit with their thoughts until they take shape, unbothered by stillness because it’s the only place that feels honest. This part is for them.
