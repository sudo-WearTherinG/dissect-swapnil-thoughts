Paid in Promises, Living on Hope

Maybe The Answers I Seek Are Not Here.

What “Success” Means Here vs There? They say you made it if you have the car, the title, the lifestyle. But that’s only here. That’s success in a world built on illusions. What about there, in the after-life, if it’s even true? What if success for the soul isn’t about what you gain, but about what you no longer need? What if it’s about walking away lighter, emptier, freer? If you still need to hold on to something, if you’re still bound, maybe you’re coming back here again, until you learn to let go.

And if the point of life here is just chasing comfort, power, and recognition, I want no part of it. The material world offers nothing worth running for anymore. I’m looking for something bigger, even if it costs me everything. Maybe it’s not even in this world.

When you feel like everything you desire is pointless and leads to nothing real, that’s when the soul stops wanting and leaves with no attachments. That’s the real win.

What if You’re Close to the End? Sometimes I wonder... maybe the reason I’m even thinking like this, questioning all of it, is because I’m close. Not close like dying, but close to understanding. Close to cracking the code. What if the moment you start asking “why am I here?” that’s when you’re finally ready to leave?

I know, it might sound like a fairytale. Something that doesn’t quite fit with the reality we know. But there’s this feeling I can’t shake. A quiet truth, almost hidden, that makes me think maybe it's not all just random. Maybe the real question isn’t about what you’ve done in this life, but about what you’re ready to let go of. And if you think this is just a delusion of mine, then explain this: why do we keep gods in our homes? Why pray, offer, and hold onto something if none of it matters?


Why Are You Here? Spoiler: They Decided

There’s only one way into this world. You’re born without choosing, thrown into a world already running, already decided. They hand you a name, teach you how to sit, smile, obey. And before you even understand what you are, they’ve already mapped out your life: school, job, marriage, bills, maybe a vacation or two if you’re lucky, then die quietly. That’s what they call normal.

But why does normal feel so hollow? Why are there a thousand ways to leave this world but only one way to enter it? Why does waking up every day feel like playing a part you never auditioned for? And deep down, why does everything feel like survival, not living?

You scroll, you work, you laugh when you’re supposed to, but it’s like something real is missing. And no one talks about it. Maybe that’s why we pray. Not because we’re devout, but because some part of us is tired. Tired of pretending this is enough. Tired of not knowing if it ever gets better. Tired of being here and not knowing why.

Tell me, why do you exist other than following a script which they handed you?

One question. One answer. One life. One soul.
