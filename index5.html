Paid in Promises, Living on Hope

Maybe The Answers I Seek Are Not Here.

What “Success” Means Here vs There? They say you made it if you have the car, the title, the lifestyle. Success here usually means gaining more: more money, more comfort, more things to call your own. But I kept wondering whether success here and success beyond here would even mean the same thing, if there even is a beyond. What if success for the soul isn't measured by what you collect, but by what you no longer need? What if it means walking away lighter instead of heavier? And if you're still holding on, still bound to something unfinished, maybe that's why people say we return. Not as punishment. Just repetition. Until something finally learns how to let go.

And if life is only about comfort, power, status, and collecting things to prove you've succeeded, then something in me pulls away from it. Not because those things are meaningless, but because they stopped feeling large enough for the questions I kept asking. I'm looking for something bigger, even if I don't know exactly what it is. Maybe it's not even here.

Sometimes I wonder whether peace begins when wanting slowly loses its grip on you. Not because desire disappears completely, but because you stop expecting every answer to come from something outside yourself. Maybe the real victory isn't gaining everything.

Maybe the thing I'm searching for doesn't fit inside the goals people spend their lives chasing.

What if You’re Close to the End? Sometimes I wonder if the reason I'm thinking like this isn't because I'm getting closer to answers, but because I finally stopped running from the questions. Maybe asking "why am I here?" isn't proof that you've reached the end. Maybe it's just the first moment you realize you've been searching all along.

I know, it might sound like a fairytale. Something that doesn’t quite fit with the reality we know. But there’s this feeling I can’t shake. A quiet truth, almost hidden, that makes me think maybe it's not all just random. Maybe the real question isn’t about what you’ve done in this life, but about what you’re ready to let go of.

Maybe all of this is just me reaching for meaning where there isn't any. I don't know. But I keep wondering why humans have always reached toward something beyond themselves. Gods, prayers, rituals, faith. Maybe people aren't searching for certainty. Maybe uncertainty simply becomes too heavy to carry alone.

And maybe that's where another question begins. If letting go matters so much, how much of what I call "myself" was ever chosen in the first place?


Why Are You Here? Spoiler: They Decided

There’s only one way into this world. None of us choose to arrive here. We enter a world already moving, already filled with rules, expectations, and stories that began before we did. You get a name before you understand identity. Expectations arrive before choice does. Before you even know who you are, ideas about success, failure, love, work, and purpose are already waiting for you: school, job, marriage, bills, maybe a vacation or two if you’re lucky, then die quietly. That’s what they call normal.

But why does normal feel so hollow? Why are there a thousand ways to leave this world but only one way to enter it? Why does waking up every day feel like playing a part you never auditioned for? And deep down, why does everything feel like survival, not living?

You scroll, you work, you laugh when you’re supposed to, but it’s like something real is missing. And no one talks about it. Maybe that's one reason people pray. Not always devotion. Sometimes exhaustion. Sometimes hope. Sometimes simply wanting to believe someone or something is listening.

Because people get tired.

Tired of pretending this is enough. Tired of wondering if it ever gets better. Tired of being here without understanding why.

Tell me, how much of your life did you actually choose, and how much arrived before you even knew you could question it?

One question. Endless answers. One consciousness trying to understand why it was written.
