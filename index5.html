Maybe The Answers I Seek Are Not Here.

What “Success” Means Here vs Beyond?

People say you’ve made it if you have the car, the title, the lifestyle. Success here usually means gaining more: more money, more comfort, more things to call your own. But I kept wondering whether success here and success beyond would even mean the same thing, if there even is a beyond.

What if success isn’t about what you collect, but what you no longer need? What if walking away lighter says more than arriving heavier?

If life is only about comfort, power, status, and proving you've succeeded through what you own, then something in me pulls away from it. Not because those things are meaningless, but because they stopped feeling large enough for the questions I kept asking. I’m looking for something bigger, even if I can’t fully explain what that means yet.

Sometimes I wonder whether peace begins when wanting slowly loses its grip on you. Not because desire disappears completely, but because you stop expecting every missing piece of yourself to come from something outside you.


What Remains After You’re Gone?

That's where my distance begins. Not because money is meaningless. Life becomes harder without it.

But I keep thinking about how temporary all of this is. We arrive here, spend years building lives, collecting things, gathering titles, trying to become someone.

Then one day we disappear, and everything we spent years building suddenly belongs to time instead of us.

And the strange part is the world doesn’t disappear with us.

It keeps moving. People continue going to work. Trains still run. Conversations continue. New people arrive. Life quietly closes the space we left behind and keeps going like we were never required for it to continue.

People remember you for a while. Then fewer people do. Then fewer than that.

And after enough time passes, one question remains: what proof remains that we were ever here at all?

Some questions refuse to stay quiet.

Sometimes I think the reason I’m thinking like this isn’t because I’m getting closer to answers, but because I finally stopped running from the questions. Asking "why am I here?" isn’t proof that you’ve reached the end. Maybe it’s the first moment you realize you’ve been searching all along.

Maybe the world is simply too loud for the quieter questions to be noticed. There’s always something demanding our attention: work, ambition, responsibility, entertainment, survival. The quieter questions wait patiently beneath all of it, and most of us don't hear them until life becomes quiet enough, or painful enough, to listen.

But there's this feeling I can't shake. Something quiet underneath all of it refuses to let me believe this is completely random. The question that keeps returning isn't what I've accomplished, but what I'm ready to let go of.

I sometimes question whether I'm reaching for meaning where there isn't any. Maybe I’m wrong.

I keep wondering why humans have always reached for something beyond themselves: gods, prayers, rituals, faith.

Maybe we aren't searching for certainty at all. Uncertainty simply becomes too heavy to carry alone.

That's where another question begins. If letting go matters so much, how much of what I call "myself" was ever chosen in the first place?


Why Are You Here? Spoiler: It Was Decided

There’s only one way into this world. None of us choose to arrive here. We enter a world already moving, already filled with rules, expectations, and stories that began before we did.

The moment we enter the world, we also enter a process of social conditioning. Long before we're capable of thinking for ourselves, we're already absorbing the world around us. Parents shape us. Society reinforces those lessons. Education organizes them. Culture surrounds us with them. By the time we're capable of questioning any of it, the world already feels normal. We adapt to it, learn to belong within it, and gradually become like everyone else around us.

You get a name before you understand identity. Expectations arrive before choice does. Long before you know who you are, ideas about success, failure, love, work, and purpose are already waiting for you: school, job, marriage, bills, maybe a vacation or two if you’re lucky, then die.

By then, it simply feels normal. The strange thing is that most of us never stop to ask who defined "normal" in the first place.

Maybe that's one reason people pray. Not always devotion. Sometimes it's exhaustion. Sometimes it's hope. Sometimes simply wanting to believe someone or something is listening.

Because people get tired.

Tired of pretending this is enough. Tired of wondering if it ever gets better. Tired of being here without understanding why.

It could be why I keep searching. But something in me still refuses to believe that's all there is.

Tell me, how much of your life did you actually choose, and how much arrived before you even knew you could question it?

I still don't know the answer. I only know I can't stop asking.

One question. Endless answers. One consciousness trying to understand why it was written.
