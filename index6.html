The Outsider’s Diary

Silent Observer On The Bench.

I know how people look at me, how their eyes judge without saying a word, assuming I’m just another spoiled rich kid who doesn’t need to work. I’ve seen those looks everywhere. It’s easy for people to label someone from the outside instead of trying to understand the things happening beneath the surface.

People build simple versions of others in their heads because judgment is easier than understanding.

They don’t understand what it’s like growing up in a house where certain things slowly change the way you see people, safety, and yourself. And then one day, without ever speaking about it, things are expected to go back to normal afterward. After a while, you begin feeling distant from a world everyone else seems to move through naturally.

I prefer being alone, surrounded by music, or spending time with little kids. Maybe because they haven't learned how to perform yet. They still say what they mean. They still exist without carrying a thousand masks. And maybe part of me stays close to that because, deep down, I know I'll never have the chance to have my own child. Some things you don't pass on. This is one of them.

Kids ask questions people stop asking once they grow older. They laugh without checking who’s watching. They cry without hiding it. Somewhere along the way adults learn how to perform themselves so well that even they forget where the performance began.

I watch as people chase money, status, attention, and followers, hoping it will fill the void inside. But I’m simply not interested in that. Instead, I chase after bonds and true connections: something deeper and more meaningful.

In this world, I exist somewhere at the edges. Close enough to watch, far enough not to belong. I never felt much attachment to the noise, the rush, or the endless race people move through without questioning.

Maybe that’s why I slowly became more comfortable watching life than fully stepping into it.

Somewhere along the way, participating started feeling heavier than observing. Sometimes it feels less like I’m living and more like I’m watching everyone else from a small distance away.

People move through routines, relationships, and expectations so naturally, while I keep stopping to question things they seem to accept without effort. Maybe everyone else learned how to belong here while some part of me never did.

But understanding people isn't always the same as feeling connected to them.

Even celebrations sometimes feel strange to me. Birthdays, festivals, traditions, the same moments returning year after year. Maybe people find comfort in repetition and shared rituals. I don't judge it. I just never felt the same connection.

Even happiness feels distant to me, like I’m watching it through glass.

I know people feel something real there. I just don’t always feel it reaching me the same way.

Most people never see this side of me. The part that questions everything. The part that quietly steps back and watches instead of following. I stopped worrying much about how people see me because their version of me usually feels incomplete anyway. But even then, I spent years adjusting parts of myself so people would find me easier to understand. After a while, I stopped knowing where adaptation ended and where I began.

I replay possibilities constantly. Conversations before they happen. Reactions before they arrive. Outcomes before choices are even made. Sometimes life starts feeling familiar before it happens, like I’ve already walked through pieces of it in my own head. It isn't always accurate. People still surprise me. But patterns repeat often enough that discovery sometimes starts feeling replaced by prediction.

The next part is something I can’t just share openly. It’s tied to my past, to the parts of me that made me who I am. It's the truth beneath everything: the reason I am the way I am. This isn’t something everyone should know, only those who’ve been there, who’ve seen the unspoken side. If you’ve ever truly understood me, you’ll know what this is about.

But if we’ve shared a real conversation, whether in person or through a social media app, I know you can unlock it. It’s not just one password; there are multiple, each for those who’ve meant more than a fleeting connection. If you’ve been a true friend, then you’ve earned the right to see what lies behind this lock.

So I’ll sit here, quiet and still, on this old bench while the world keeps moving around me. I don't need to be seen. I just needed proof that I was here. The world kept moving when I sat down here, and maybe it'll keep moving long after I stand up too. But at least I know I existed inside it for a little while.

Maybe life was never about fully belonging anywhere. Maybe some people are only meant to observe, to question, to notice the things others learn to ignore. At some point, loneliness and clarity started feeling almost identical.

After all the noise, the fights, and the silence, I’m still here, still myself, still chasing what no one else sees.
