The Outsider’s Diary

Silent Observer On The Bench.

I know how people look at me, how their eyes judge without saying a word: thinking I’m just another spoiled, rich kid who doesn’t need to work. I’ve seen those eyes everywhere I go. It’s easy to make assumptions, but truth isn’t always what it seems. They don’t know my past at all.

They don’t see the weight I carry. They don’t get what it’s like to be raised inside something that broke you and then be expected to continue living as if it didn’t. They don’t understand what it feels like to become detached from a world built on patterns and scripts everyone else seems to follow naturally. I prefer being alone, surrounded by music, or spending time with little kids. Maybe because they haven’t learned how to perform yet. They still say what they mean. They still exist without carrying a thousand masks. And maybe part of me stays close to that because, deep down, I know I’ll never have the chance to have my own child.

I watch as people chase money, status, attention, and followers, hoping it will fill the void inside. But I’m simply not interested in that. It’s not the path I want to follow. Instead, I chase after bonds and true connections: something deeper and more meaningful.

In this world, I exist somewhere at the edges. Close enough to watch, far enough not to belong. I never felt much attachment to the noise, the rush, or the endless race people move through without questioning. Somewhere along the way, observing became easier than participating. Not because I think I’m above it, but because pretending I fit into a world that never felt natural to me became exhausting.

I was never meant to live like a machine. I came here, studied how humans live, the routines, the roles people inherit, the noise everyone learns to survive inside. Somewhere along the way I became less interested in participating and more interested in understanding life. Sometimes I wonder if everyone else got used to living here while some part of me never did. I can understand people. I can study them. But understanding something isn't always the same as feeling connected to it.

I never felt drawn toward the things people spend their lives chasing. Money, status, appearances, the milestones that seem important to everyone else. Even celebrations sometimes feel strange to me. Birthdays, festivals, traditions, the same moments returning year after year. Maybe people find comfort in repetition and shared rituals. I don't judge it. I just never felt the same connection. Sometimes happiness itself feels distant too, like I’m watching it through glass. I know people feel something real there. I just don’t always feel it reaching me the same way.

Most people never see this side of me. The part that questions everything. The part that quietly steps back and watches instead of following. I stopped worrying much about how people see me because their version of me usually feels incomplete anyway. But even then, I spent years adjusting parts of myself so people would find me easier to understand. After a while, I stopped knowing where adaptation ended and where I began.

I replay possibilities constantly. Conversations before they happen. Reactions before they arrive. Outcomes before choices are even made. Sometimes life starts feeling familiar before it happens, like I’ve already walked through pieces of it in my own head. It isn't always accurate. People still surprise me. But patterns repeat often enough that discovery sometimes starts feeling replaced by prediction.

The next part is something I can’t just share openly. It’s tied to my past, to the parts of me that made me who I am. It's the truth beneath everything: the reason I am the way I am. This isn’t something everyone should know, only those who’ve been there, who’ve seen the unspoken side. If you’ve ever truly understood me, you’ll know what this is about.

But if we’ve shared a real conversation, whether in person or through a social media app, I know you can unlock it. It’s not just one password; there are multiple, each for those who’ve meant more than a fleeting connection. If you’ve been a true friend, then you’ve earned the right to see what lies behind this lock.

So I’ll sit here, quiet and still, on this old bench while the world keeps moving around me. I don't need to be seen. I just needed proof that I was here. The world kept moving when I sat down here, and maybe it'll keep moving long after I stand up too. But at least I know I existed inside it for a little while.

After all the noise, the fights, and the silence, I’m still here, still myself, still chasing what no one else sees.
